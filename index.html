▸ what do these do?
TWR – power & punch (TWR:1 thrust-to-weight). Higher = more climb/accel.
Rate – top rotation speed (°/s). Higher = faster flips/rolls.
Snap – how fast it reaches that rate. Higher = twitchier, less rotational inertia.
Mass – inertia. Heavier = carries more momentum, glides, harder to stop.
Drag – air resistance. Lower = higher top speed & more float; higher = planted.
Throttle resp – motor spool lag (ms). Lower = punchier vertical.
Gravity – fall strength (9.81 = real). Raise if it feels floaty.
P – stiffness of rate hold. I – corrects steady drift. D – damping/smoothness (cuts overshoot).
Roll sensitivity – roll rate vs pitch. Expo – softens stick centre for fine control.
Tip: more inertia = Mass↑ + Drag↓. Snappier = Rate↑ & Snap↑. Smoother = D↑, Expo↑.
